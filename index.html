LearnASM: An educational assembly code simulator in your web browser

LearnASM allows users to not only program assembly right in their web browser, but also to get inside the CPU and see what is really happening. In the future, LearnASM will support a "Learn" mode to teach novice programmers assembly with the goal of teaching them how to break down steps of a program into tiny pieces for a computer.

About

Hi, I'm Nathan Pennie, aka KB1RD! I work as the Chief Technology Officer for New England Sci-Tech and I am a founding member of the Sci-Tech Youth Amateur Radio Society. (STARS) I have an Amateur Extra class radio license and enjoy working with computers and electronics. I use Linux as my primary OS and I am a supporter of the Free Software Movement. In my free time I code (mostly C++) and I design electronic circuits, most of the time for New England Sci-Tech kits. You won't find me on the air often, mostly because I don't have a HF radio. If you'd like to talk to me, you can often find me at New England Sci-Tech for the Tuesday night STARS meetings.
